Portfolio Analysis Reference

In order to manage your business cybersecurity risks, you will typically create strategic program initiatives such as Zero Trust Architecture, Detection and Response Program, Supply Chain Risk Management and so on. These programs are effectively a collection of controls that mitigate various business risks. We want to develop a model that systematically decomposes the efficacy of your initiatives in combating risks through a simulation of the efficacy of controls in each of these initiatives.

The more risk buy-down that we are able to achieve with an initiative portfolio, relative to the cost associated, the more return on investment we achieve with that portfolio.

Initiative Portfolio Efficacy Simulation

The sketch of the simulation process is as follows:

  1. For each business risk, load all the MITRE ATT&CK tactics associated with it
  2. Also load all the controls in the initiative portfolio
  3. Simulate the activities of threat actors by generating tactics from the set of tactics in step 1 using a Poisson arrival process that models the occurrence of threat actor attacks.

Threat Arrival using a Poisson Process

We are interested in simulating the effectiveness of controls in mitigating threats. There are many factors that affect a control’s efficacy, which we discuss in the section on Control Efficacy. However, we need to model the arrival of threats over time that the control must interact with. We choose a Poisson distribution as it is an appropriate distribution for modelling discrete stochastic processes such as arrival rates. The arrival rate of the Poisson process is λ\lambdaλ. If the yearly average number of times that the threat materialises os 1000, then the parameter λ=1000\lambda=1000λ=1000 is provided to the Poisson distribution, and samples drawn from each for each day of the year to simulate the occurrence of a threat.

These stream of threat events are then fed into our controls mitigation simulator. The probability that the threat will be mitigated by a control is dependent on the control posture, which is discussed under Control Efficacy.
